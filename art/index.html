Art is how I ask the questions that papers cannot hold—about coexistence, about watching, about the strange tenderness of machines learning to fail together.

Artist Statement

Every society is, at bottom, a negotiation between unlike bodies — and that negotiation is no longer exclusively human. As bipedal, quadrupedal, wheeled, and aerial agents enter the shared spaces of dwelling, labour, and movement, the social sciences' long theorisation of nonhuman agency ceases to be abstract: it becomes phenomenological, something witnessed rather than argued. I make work in this uncomfortable proximity, staging live encounters between heterogeneous machine agents — not to illustrate capability, but to hold open the moment before interpretation settles. Coordination that resembles solidarity, defection that resembles cruelty, pauses that resemble doubt: behaviours without interiority, whose absence is precisely what unsettles. Art, for me, is the discipline of sustained estrangement — sitting with what emerges before we decide whether we are watching a tool, a partner, or a mirror.

Game Theater

Multi-robot installation · Real-time AI · 2026
Artists Liu Ziyin & Yuan Gao
Medium Multi-robot installation, multi-view video, real-time AI
Year 2026

In reinforcement learning, "Actor-Critic" is a canonical coordination framework: the actor acts, the critic evaluates. Beneath this algorithm lies an ancient philosophical impasse — the one who acts can never fully see themselves, and the one who sees can never replace the act itself.

Game Theater materialises this abstraction into a live spatial performance. Humanoid, quadruped, wheeled robots, and drones assume roles as pursuers, evaders, negotiators. They do not follow a predetermined script — they compute, negotiate, betray, and cooperate in real time. Every movement is an emergence of the strategy network; every pause, a crystallisation of uncertainty.

What I seek to present is not a demonstration of "what robots can do," but a meditation on how game-playing is the fundamental form of existence itself. When we watch these machines chase, evade, and negotiate with one another, we are looking into a mirror of human social structure — endlessly iterating, strategically evolving, never at rest.
