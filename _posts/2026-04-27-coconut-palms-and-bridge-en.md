![Coconut Palms at Sunset]({{ site.url }}/images/blog/2026-04/palm_sunset_1.jpg)

The sky poured its last gold onto the sea.

The coconut palms stood there, saying nothing, merely stretching their shadows long. The long bridge lay across the water and sky, like a frozen wave—no one knew where it led, or perhaps it didn't need to lead anywhere.

![Bridge at Dusk]({{ site.url }}/images/blog/2026-04/palm_sunset_2.jpg)

Someone spread a blanket on the grass and flattened themselves into the shape of the character for "big." The sky was still bright, the water still glowing, and in the distance someone rode by on a bike, their wheels rolling over patches of light as if rolling over a shattered Milky Way.

The wind came from the sea, passed through the gaps in the palm fronds, making a delicate rustling sound. It didn't sound like music—it sounded more like breathing—breathing without a conductor, a thousand people with a thousand rhythms.

![Cycling by the Shore]({{ site.url }}/images/blog/2026-04/palm_sunset_3.jpg)

The setting sun dyed everything in the same color. The grass was gold, the water was gold, the bridge was gold, and even those lying flat people were gilded with a thin layer of warmth.

No one looked up at the sky. But the sky knew. It kept its best light for those who weren't in a hurry.

![Sunset Melting into Gold]({{ site.url }}/images/blog/2026-04/palm_sunset_4.jpg)

The long bridge was still there.

It said nothing. It promised nothing. It simply stood there, letting what should pass pass, letting what should come come.

*Coconut palms line up, opening the sea and sky;*
*Ten thousand li of clouds enter the heroic heart.*
*Do not speak of many obstacles on the journey—*
*The east wind fills the sleeves, spring has arrived.*
